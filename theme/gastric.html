Gastric Alimetryᵀᴹ For Kids


Project purpose

All kids get an occasional stomach ache — think not enough sleep or too much Halloween candy. However, when they occur frequently, they can be brought on by a variety of factors, including infections, bacteria, viruses and anxiety.

The diagnosis has never been easy because children find it challenging to adequately articulate their symptoms. Even some parents feel uncertain on how to address their child’s pain as well.

The brief

Our client wants to develop a paediatrics version of Alimetryᵀᴹ to help doctors diagnose. Gastric Alimetryᵀᴹ technology non-invasively senses the motility of the stomach to detect the underlying mechanisms of gastric symptoms, informing personalized therapy.

The task is to redsigned the severity app for children, aged 5-17 years. To enable a young person to record gastric symptoms once a day, for 7 days before clinic day for further testing.

The APP needs to be clear and simple for the little ones to use. The information data can be captured and recorded for research analyses. The app should be based on the adult version with the same logo and branding colour.

Gastric Alimetryᵀᴹ

is a non-invasive device, worn in the comfort of the clinic.
The device delivers new validated biomarkers of gastric function, based on multi-award-winning scientific innovation.

The challenge

Coping techniques to help kids thrive

  • Adult app currently not suitable and engaging for paediatric use
  • Children struggle to understand how to rate pain severity using a 0-10 scale
  • Children find it diificult to understand the difference between all 10 symptoms
  • Design an app that is engaging for 5-8 age group, but not boring for 9-14 age group

Define

Problem Statement ONE

The existing gastric app is being used in adults designed to track and rate the severity of their gastric symptoms, however the app is not suitable for paediatric use (5-18 years old).
Because children have a different level of health literacy, they are confused when they see a scale to rate symptoms. They are also bored with the lack of engagement within the app.
As a result, the child may not fully understand making the ratings inaccurate.

Problem Statement TWO

The current app has no ability to track symptoms in the week leading up to clinic day. This is important information as the patient could be having a “good” day when they come in for the clinic session.
Because of this, the participant tries to recall the symptoms they have had for the week and it is more difficult for children to remember.
As a result, it is unlikely the researcher can get an accurate inforamtion during the 7 days leading up to clinic day, so the diagnosis may be inaccurate.
