The Background


This is a given context design challenge.

Diana and Alan, owners of a small business, used the PB Tech website to buy new laptops (about 15 units), but they were dissatisfied with the online shopping experience.

The task is to use the given story as the context, provide a prototype for the development of redesigned laptop feature pages. Using https://www.pbtech.co.nz/category/computers/laptops as a reference..

The Problems


As the largest computer retailer in New Zealand, PB Tech's online sales performance has been less than stellar. They also want to attract more online corporate customers . But sales to business customers are not doing well either.

The Outcome


Highlight of the solution


Add a quiz feature to help users find the right laptop

Before, on the landing page, the user is only allowed to choose one category at a time. An example would be, a user selecting 'business laptop', which will result in 381 products on the next listing page. Now, by using the quiz feature, if the user selects ‘business laptop> mac> medium size’, there will only be 23 results left.

This could help narrow down the search results, so that users can find a meaningful starting point.

Quiz reduced irrelevant results by 91 %


Enrich the hero section to help corporate customers know about PB's business plans

We added the business information on the hero area of the landing page to help corporate customers understand PB-Tech’s business plan and bulk pricing.

PB-Tech‘s original website has a business plan page, which is a wonderful end-to-end business IT service, but it is not easily found.

Redesigned landing page to support different users shopping behavior

Our solution is using a hybrid approach by:

providing browsing experience to let the users see popular products right away

retaining the category approach but with a new layout, as it plays an educational role for complex products like laptops.

allowing users to skip them by redesigning the search function.

Restructured Filter to help further narrow down search results
  • Have to click "apply" every time
  • No explanation
  • List too long
  • Show results automatically to save users' efforts
  • Provide more details with tooltips
  • Clearer filtering list
Simplified Product page to help users focus on the product
  • Product name mixed with specification details
  • Description of feature is crowded and overloaded with information
  • Simplified product name
  • Gave product information more space
  • Increased the visibility of the features & specifications

Design Thinking Process

How we got there

DISCOVER

Understand the current product


Heuristic review

We conducted Heuristic review together with three developers.

The method I used is letting each evaluator inspected PB Tech’s website individually, to ensure independent and unbiased evaluations. Following the completion of all evaluations, I facilitated a meeting for communication and aggregation of our findings.

Understand users’ current experience


Customer journey map

We found three users and gave them a task as a business owner looking for laptops. According to their actions combined with the given insights of customers, we created a customer journey map.

It allowed us to communicate the severity of the pain‐points and facilitate conversations about the areas we wanted to fix.

An interesting finding leads to further research

Two users selected "business laptop category" on the landing page, but then got lost when they saw more than 300 items on the following listing page.

One user firstly wanted to browse the most popular business models, but it took him a long time to find them at the very bottom of the landing page.

Understand the user‘s behaviours


Secondary research

Three users showed different behaviours ,two used category but the other one wanted to browse first. So we tried to find out the reason behind that.

Our secondary research revealed a classic user behaviour theory known as the "Users’ Berry-Picking Model" which well describes how people shop online.

So we decide to redesign the laptop landing page by using a hybrid approach including searching, browsing and category options to meet different customers’ needs.

IDEATE

Using “How Might We” Questions to Turn Insights into Opportunities


How might we help users select the right laptop easily?

1. Brainstorming

I organised a brainstorming session first and collaborated with three developers.

It is better to know the feasibility at the early stage.

It also helps us to build consensus with the developers.

working as a team



Where the idea of a quiz came from ?

We found an article on the landing page, which guides users how to select a laptop step by step.

However, none of the users discovered it, not to mention read it.

During brainstorming, I came up with the idea of converting this guide into a quiz with simple steps, because few people would read through such a long article.



Testing

I tested and discovered that if we designed the quiz into three steps:

Purposes ---> OS ---> Size

It would narrow down the number of search results by around 90%.



User flow

Before we turned it into a prototype, we made a user flow for this new feature.

PROTOTYPE

Using Crazy Eights to Turn Ideation into Prototype

Since we had so many details to design and were under time pressure, we needed to work rapidly, jumping straight from sketching to prototyping.

So I decided to use the Crazy 8 method. The purpose is to generate a number of different ideas within a short period of time.



Using the user flow as a guide, we did our own sketches using crazy 8 and rated the parts that we liked.

After discussing the feasibility with the developers, our vision began evolving into something tangible. A high‐level design language, and interactions began to piece together.



TEST & ITERATION

Testing Our Assumptions


Once the team had a prototype ready for use, we knew we needed to put it in the hands of our customers.

Usability Test

Methodology : Interview

Participant: 5 Users

Results: The test helps us verify 2 things we did well and find 3 issues to improve.

Things we did well

100% of Participants were able to successfully complete the quiz

Verified hybrid approach can meet users’ “berry-picking model”

The solution of hybrid approach works well in our user test. Three of them started with the quiz at the very beginning. The other two users, one started with browsing the most popular laptops and the other one used the search bar directly.

Things need to be improved

According to the discussion of Severity Rating, the problems below became our priority to fix.

Iteration


Redesigned up-selling information for corporate customer

So, we changed the wording of the CTA from "apply for business account" to "contact us".

Signing up is a big commitment for most people. Most users today are more wary than ever about how to handle their personal information.

Before applying for an account, they need to know more information to have trust and comfort. By clicking contact us , it will lead to PB-tech business service page. So the user can get access to the information they need regarding PB-tech's business plan and contact methods.

We added the business information on the landing page by placing it in the hero area. Because one of our testers, Janet, who had real-world experience purchasing computers for her company, had a tendency to go to the home page rather than the product page.

Reflection


At the time of submission, I had difficulty accepting the reality of this product, because I knew there were so much I could do. As it was a design challenge, I was unable to conduct stakeholder interviews .There was no opportunity to ask why.

If I had more time, I would like to investigate deeper into the needs for the users. Additionally, I want to conduct an AB test to verify our improvements. Also gathering precise data for the next iteration.

I am very proud of what we accomplished as a team. I’ve learned a lot about the perspectives from developers.

I am so thankful to have had a team that was hard working and great to collaborate with.
